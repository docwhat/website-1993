Information on DOS

DOS has been made by several companies; Microsoft, IBM, and the now bought out (by Novell) Digital Research.

Some of my favorite utilities for DOS include:

List
List is a useful tool for view text and binary files under DOS. It's also a quick way to scan directories.

Qedit
A nice easy to use editor both with WordStar key commands and a menu. I couldn't find a copy on the net, but if you find one, email me and I'll put a pointer here.

Info-ZIP's Unzip and Zip
These two tool are great compression programs, and they're much less obnoxious than Phil Katz's PKZIP's. Also, if you use UN*X at all, these are almost identical to the UN*X versions.

The main site I use for most of my DOS programs is uiarchive.cso.uiuc.edu in the directories under /pub/systems/pc
